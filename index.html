AI for Financial Access

HerStory

Young girls from underrepresented communities face two barriers to financial literacy: lack of access to quality education and lack of relatable mentors who look like them. HerStory uses AI-powered conversations with women leaders like Michelle Obama and Sheryl Sandberg to teach financial concepts in an engaging, personalized way. Users can ask questions, receive guidance, and learn from role models who understand their challenges firsthand.

Financial news moves markets, but most people can't decode how headlines translate to investment decisions. BuzzTrade analyzes social media posts and news articles to predict their impact on stock prices and investor sentiment. AI for Accessibility

Healthy Habits

Living with ADD taught me that traditional planners don't work for everyone. HealthyHabits uses AI to create personalized study schedules that accommodate learning disabilities like ADHD, dyslexia, and auditory processing disorder. Unlike typical planners, it prioritizes mental and physical health by scheduling breaks, meals, and self-care alongside academic work - because productivity means nothing without wellness.

Research

Exploring innovative solutions at the intersection of technology and social impact.

AI Companions as Non-Judgmental Financial and Mental Health Allies for Underserved Populations

Financial stress and mental health challenges form a vicious cycle, especially in low-income communities with limited access to both financial advisors and mental health care. My research explores how AI companions can provide non-judgmental, 24/7 support for both financial education and emotional wellbeing. By combining financial literacy tools with empathetic conversational AI, I'm investigating whether technology can help break the cycle where financial insecurity worsens mental health, which in turn makes financial stability harder to achieve.

Leadership & Community-Building

Empowering communities through education, mentorship, and opportunity.

Connect&Expand

Girls in my school were interested in business careers but didn't know where to start - and our community offered few opportunities for exploration. I founded Connect & Expand to bridge this gap by connecting FBLA members to professional mentors and job shadowing experiences in business and finance. So far, we've connected 4 students to mentors and are arranging 13 job shadowing opportunities.

STEMPowerHer Hackathon

The best solutions to community problems come from the people who live them. STEMPowerHer Hackathon invites girls from around the world to build AI and business solutions for challenges in their own communities - whether that's financial literacy in rural areas, healthcare access, or educational barriers. We've attracted over 85 participants from 20 countries, each bringing unique perspectives and real-world problems to solve.

STEMPowerHer International Community Journal

Many talented girls worldwide lack access to STEM education and role models in the field. The STEMPowerHer Journal showcases research, projects, and innovative ideas from young women in STEM, giving them a platform to share their work and connect with a global community. We've received nearly 30 submissions from 15+ countries across Africa, Asia, and Europe.
